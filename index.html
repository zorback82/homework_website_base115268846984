Gender equality is necessary

Posted on October 28, 2022 by Léo AJAS
Open letter Championing Equality

Today, as in the past, gender equality is a principle that is not clearly established in our societies. Much has been done and the social norm has also changed. Nevertheless, many inequalities have occurred.

In the popular culture and world of the 20th and 21st centuries, the sexist society defined the woman as an individual who had to take care of the household, the house, prepare food and manage the education of children in the family.

Instead, the man worked and brought the salary home. Therefore, the woman was clearly dependent on her husband and not economically independent. Men, on the other hand, were portrayed as strong, emotionless beings, represented in the form of courage and determination.

We must all be equal

These inequalities have diminished over the years but are still present in the 21st century. That is why, for a better world, we must fight these prejudices.

Whoever we are, man or woman, whatever our sex or gender, we should all have the same rights and be equal in our society. In a way, we are all feminists (reference to C. Ngozi Adichie).

"The two sexes are not superior or inferior to each other. They are simply different." (Gregorio Marañón)
